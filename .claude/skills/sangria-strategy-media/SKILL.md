# Sangria - Strategy & Media (conceptual layer)

The upstream thinking behind Sangria's work, across all clients. Three pillars: (1) client pitches, (2) creative concept & communication strategy, (3) media strategy. The "why" and the "approach" - not the executional "how".

## Two pitch frames (use whichever fits)
- **"Clients Deserve Better"** - better partnerships, ideas, expertise, results. The relationship-and-trust frame (strong for incumbent/relationship pitches like Copa).
- **"Above the Algo / Making people feel brands"** - data shows what people do, emotion explains why it matters; measure the numbers you can see and the feelings you can't fake (the newer brand frame).

## Scope boundary
Conceptual only. The moment the work becomes a concrete media plan, insertion order, trafficking sheet, performance report, analytics build, or attribution model, switch to **sangria-agency-os**. Keep numbers, pacing %, CPMs/CPAs, line items, UTMs, dashboards, and attribution OUT of anything produced here.

## How to use
Identify the pillar, read the matching file first, then write. Most tasks touch two pillars (a pitch contains a strategy; a concept implies a media role). All files live flat in this folder.

| If the task is about... | Read |

## Sangria's upstream principles
- **Lead with the relationship (when you have one).** For incumbents, reframe the RFP's real question ("are you with the right partner?") and answer it with shared history, trust, and proof.
- **Brilliant Basics first.** Excellence at the fundamentals (flawless setup, precise optimization, advanced tracking) underwrites the big ideas - never the other way around.
- **Evidence first.** Ground every claim and target in real data (brand-equity waves, prior results, category structure, platform benchmarks, research partners like Mintel/Kantar).
- **Honest, fiduciary voice.** "Agency comes from Agent." Transparency, no hidden fees, act in the client's interest; set expectations explicitly; be willing to stake part of compensation on the outcome.
- **Own the open territory.** Pick a space no competitor owns; for an underdog, push Share of Voice above Market Share with distinctive assets.
- **Two-motor demand.** Always balance demand capture (catch intent) with demand creation (build intent).
- **Every channel has one defined role.** No redundancy; each fills a specific funnel gap and states its contribution.
- **Sequence the message.** Educate then persuade then close; audiences cold then engaged then intent; big moments as announcement waves.
- **Tailor to where the audience already is.** Don't sell people what they already have; segment the message by current status/relationship.
- **Articulate the brief as GET / WHO / TO / BY.**
- **Concept first, adapt by format.** Develop the idea in one language, then adapt at the format/market level.
- **Plan in scenarios.** Conservative / Target / Stretch with the logic behind each, never as promises.
