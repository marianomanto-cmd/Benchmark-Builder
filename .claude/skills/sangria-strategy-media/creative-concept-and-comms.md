# Creative concept & communication strategy

## What a concept is
A single creative idea or territory that organizes a campaign's communication *before* any format/production. Concept docs are often named `CLIENT.c####.[Name]Concept` (e.g. Buy Miles Concept, US Positioning Concept, World Cup Concept, Stopover Creative).

## The concept workflow
- Develop the concept in **one language first** (typically English), as a clean idea - not localized executions.
- Iterate over roughly **two rounds of client feedback**.
- **Language/market adaptations happen later, at the format level** - not inside the concept.
- Concepts are often **inspired by/evolved from prior winning creatives** the client shares.
- Commercial wrapper = a creative-development SOW (pricing in sangria-agency-os/commercial).

## Articulate the brief: GET / WHO / TO / BY
- **GET** [audience] **WHO** [insight/desire] **TO** [behavior] **BY** [the offer/idea].
- Example (Stopover): GET curious travelers WHO want to add excitement and variety to their trips TO discover a new destination BY booking a Panama Stopover with Copa.

## The communication spine (the Copa Campaign Brief)
Background/Overview (situation, competitive landscape, market share, sales trends, load factor, seasonality) - **Challenge** - **Objective** (Awareness / Consideration / Engagement / Performance) - **Campaign Type** (Positioning / New Destination / Destination / Products / Sponsorships) - **Strategy Category** (Origin / Ofertas que vuelan / Coupon Code / Tourism Authorities / New Destinations / Branding / Promotions / Destination / News & Communication) - **Target Audience** (demo + psychographic + behavior + contextual + retargeting) - Markets & Languages - Landing - **a key strategic inspiration/benchmark to guide the concept** - Message - **RTBs** - mandatories. (Full field list in sangria-agency-os/intake-and-briefs.)

## Build the audience as a persona + a mindset journey
- **Persona** (e.g. "The Curious Explorer"): demographics, origin markets, purpose mix, average stay, activities/interests, program awareness, motivations ("two-for-one vacation"), potential needs. Name it; make it human.
- **Mindset journey**: map **Attention -> Intent -> Influence** against the traveler's path **Dreaming -> Planning -> Sharing -> Booking -> Experiencing**, and align that to the **Awareness -> Consideration -> Performance** funnel. Each phase gets the message that matches the mindset.

## Positioning concepts
Define the territory to own; prefer an **open territory no competitor owns** (e.g. Felix -> "Convenience"). For an Outsider brand, push **Share of Voice > Market Share** with distinctive assets. Positioning campaigns recur per market (Copa US / Colombia / DR / Puerto Rico / Panama). A reframe can be the concept itself - e.g. Stopover repositioned from "a layover" into "the thrill of discovery" (idle transit -> a reason to fly), or **conquesting** (seize a competitor's repositioning - Colombia vs Avianca).

## Segment-aware concept (tailor to where they are)
Don't sell people what they already have. Example - ConnectMiles co-brand card (CMI): the "deserved status" angle fits prospects but NOT existing PreferMembers; for them lean on PQM accumulation and incremental benefits. Build the value-prop per tier (PreferMembers / Active Members / Members / Flyers / Hispanics). Make benefits ladder up to a feeling. Creative platform example: **"Connect to what matters most" + "Earn that feeling"**.

## Dual-pillar messaging
Brief two parallel tracks and compare/test: a **personality/authority track** (e.g. Patrick Bet-David on camera) vs a **product-led track** (the tangible product/offer). Decide or A/B which resonates.

## Message arc across the funnel
Educate -> persuade -> close. Patterns: Valuetainment "**Who is PBD -> Why The Vault -> Urgency/FOMO**" (educate about the person first); airline "destination desire -> fare/offer -> CTA urgency". Plan big moments (reveals, offers, announcements) as **waves**.

## Tentpole / cultural tie-in concepts
Hook the concept to a cultural moment when there's audience overlap. Example - **Stopover x World Cup 2026**: "make a free Stopover in Panama (up to 7 days) on your way to support your national team", targeting football and World-Cup-country fans Copa flies. The tie-in becomes an alternate execution layered onto the base campaign.

## Creator-led concepts
The creator economy rewards **long-term, authentic partnerships** (shared values, creative freedom, creators involved throughout). Owned vs creator content gaps are large; video-first, and TikTok is often the most compelling for discovery + authenticity + intent-based search. Two activation shapes:
- **Branded Mission** - preselected creators work a brand brief; platform guarantees featuring + a centralizing hashtag page + premium FYP placement; full-funnel (explain -> benefits -> book).
- **UGC Challenge** - incentivize a wider community to post via the creator marketplace; cost-effective amplification and content generation.
Pair with a **Brand Lift study** to read perception/share-of-mind (ad recall, awareness, favorability, intent).

## Ideas beyond the budget
Show ambition past the brief with an idea bank (e.g. Stopover Ultimate Experience: AI assistant/chatbot, personalized referral program, experiential/QR spots, memory maker, merchandising, audio content), marking which are included vs aspirational.

## Platform-native creative (principle)
Each platform gets tailored formats - TikTok != Instagram != YouTube != LinkedIn. Authentic/direct-to-camera often beats polished. (Specs/best-practices in sangria-agency-os/creative.)

## Concept -> pillars
Name **3-6 message pillars** and keep names consistent downstream so performance reads by message theme. Airline pillar example: onboard service, connectivity, punctuality, business class. (Pillar mechanics in sangria-agency-os/creative.)
